Social basics

  • Social rules - Recurse Center
  • No phones out during class. First-come, first-served access to the ten slot Phone Spa. All other phones must stay in your pocket or bag during class or will be taken and not charged.
  • Computers are for programming and research related to your work. Don’t play games, don’t go on social media, etc.

Grades

Portfolio (~40%)

  • The collection of all the code you wrote over the year.
  • You will have an opportunity at the end of the year to go back and work on earlier projects.
  • You will also get to pick a small number of programs as the best representation of your work.

Journal (~40%)

  • Daily journal of what you worked on, what problems you ran into, and how you got unstuck.

How you work (~20%)

  • Being present, both literally and figuratively.
  • Staying on task, even when it’s hard. (“If you’re not stuck, you’re not programming.”)
  • Being helpful to your peers and sharing what you learn.
  • Not being disruptive to others.

Homework

  • Very little to none.
  • You can work on your programs outside of class but it should not be necessary.

Coding, open source, and working together

  • Write your own code.
  • But definitely do talk to your peers about your code and theirs.
  • If you find useful snippets of code on the internet, cite it in a comment.

Mechanics

  • No food near computers.
  • Masks encouraged per school policy.
